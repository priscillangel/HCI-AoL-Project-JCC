About JCC

Jockey’s Conference Center (JCC) stands proudly as a premier destination for conferences, exhibitions, and events in Indonesia’s bustling capital city, Jakarta. With its rich history and remarkable achievements, this world-class facility has firmly established itself as a symbol of Jakarta’s prominence on the global stage.

Inaugurated in 1974, Jockey’s Conference Center reflects the ambitious vision of the Indonesian government to establish a state-of-the-art venue for national and international events. Designed by the renowned architect, Friedrich Silaban, the center’s architecture combines modern aesthetic with traditional Indonesian elements, creating an iconic structure that showcases the country’s cultural heritage.

JCC's Achievement

Since its inception, Jockey’s Conference Center has been a hub for significant national and international events, contributing to the growth and development of Jakarta’s business and tourism sectors. Its versatile facilities and professional services have earned it a distinguished reputation among event organizers and participants worldwide.

Some of JCC’s notable achievements:

1. Global Conference Hub

JCC has hosted numerous prestigious global conferences, including high-level governmental summits, regional business forums, and international trade exhibitions. Its world-class facilities, state-of-the-art audiovisual technology, and spacious halls have provided an ideal platform for meaningful discussions, knowledge exchange, and networking.

2. Award-Winning Venue

JCC has received multiple awards and accolades for its excellence in event management and facility design. These recognitions are a testament to the center's commitment to delivering exceptional experiences to event organizers and attendees alike.

3. Promoting Local Industries

JCC has played a crucial role in promoting Indonesian industries and supporting economic growth. It has hosted large-scale exhibitions showcasing the country's diverse sectors, including manufacturing, agriculture, tourism, and creative industries. These exhibitions have provided a platform for businesses to showcase their products and services to local and international audiences.

4. Community Engagement

JCC actively engages with the local community by organizing various public events and cultural festivals. These initiatives foster a sense of community and celebrate Indonesia's rich cultural heritage, providing opportunities for people to come together and appreciate the nation's artistic and creative endeavors.

5. Sustainability Initiatives

JCC is committed to environmental sustainability and has implemented numerous initiatives to reduce its ecological footprint. From eco-friendly waste management systems to energy-efficient lighting, the center aims to minimize its impact on the environment and inspire others to follow suit.
